Early Life and College Career

Lamar Jackson's football journey began long before he became a household name in the NFL. Growing up in Florida, Jackson’s athletic ability was evident early on. He became a standout player at Boynton Beach High School before committing to play college football at the University of Louisville. In his sophomore year, Jackson led the Louisville Cardinals to a spectacular season, earning the Heisman Trophy for his outstanding performance.

His college career set the stage for his entry into the NFL. With impressive stats and leadership, Jackson’s dual-threat abilities as both a passer and rusher made him an exciting prospect for NFL teams. His college success was a precursor to his groundbreaking professional career.

Professional Career

  • Drafted by the Baltimore Ravens in 2018 NFL Draft
  • Set NFL Record for Most Rushing Yards by a Quarterback
  • Led the Ravens to multiple playoff appearances
  • Super Bowl Contender for Future Seasons

Jackson's professional career began in 2018 when he was selected 32nd overall in the first round by the Baltimore Ravens. In his rookie season, he showcased his electrifying running ability, eventually taking over as the starting quarterback. In 2019, he delivered a historic MVP season, leading the NFL in passing touchdowns while setting records for rushing yards by a quarterback.
